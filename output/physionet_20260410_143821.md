## Summary

PhysioNet functions as a key research resource for accessing, sharing, and reusing complex physiologic health signals. The platform has recently updated its policies to comply with strict U.S. Department of Justice regulations concerning the transfer of sensitive data to certain countries or entities. Furthermore, the site issues critical guidelines for using sensitive datasets, such as MIMIC, with Large Language Models, mandating local deployment and ensuring zero data retention to maintain data integrity.

## Key Facts

* PhysioNet updated access policies due to the U.S. Department of Justice's Data Security Program (DSP) compliance.
* The DSP final rule took effect on April 8, 2025, with full enforcement beginning July 8, 2025.
* DSP restrictions apply to sharing or transferring bulk sensitive personal data (e.g., genomic, biometric, health) with specified countries or "covered persons."
* PhysioNet prevents access to certain datasets from IP addresses or affiliations in restricted regions, including China, Cuba, Iran, North Korea, Russia, and Venezuela.
* The use of credentialed and restricted data like MIMIC-III and MIMIC-IV with LLMs/online services is prohibited by the PhysioNet Data Use Agreement.
* MIMIC data must not be stored or retained by third-party LLM services.
* Researchers are strongly recommended to use locally deployed LLMs when working with MIMIC data.
* Roger G. Mark and the late George B. Moody were co-recipients of the 2026 IEEE Biomedical Engineering Award.
* Roger G. Mark is the co-founder of PhysioNet, which launched in 1999 to provide open access to physiologic signals and clinical data.
* A Nature Health paper highlighted PhysioNet's role as a global platform for biomedical research over 25 years.

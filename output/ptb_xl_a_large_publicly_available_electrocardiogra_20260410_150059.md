## Summary

The page details the PTB-XL dataset, a comprehensive and publicly available resource for electrocardiography (ECG) interpretation. This dataset comprises 21,799 clinical 12-lead ECG recordings from thousands of patients, making it ideal for developing diagnostic support systems. Its richness stems from extensive annotations by cardiologists, which cover 71 different ECG statements across diagnostic, form, and rhythm aspects. Complementing the signal data is detailed metadata on patient demographics and cardiac conditions. Overall, PTB-XL serves as a critical benchmark resource for training and evaluating automated algorithms in the field of cardiology.

## Key Facts

*   **Dataset Name and Purpose:** PTB-XL is a large, publicly available electrocardiography (ECG) dataset designed for training and evaluating automatic ECG interpretation algorithms.
*   **Number of Records:** It contains 21,799 clinical 12-lead ECG records.
*   **Number of Patients:** The dataset comprises data from 18,869 patients.
*   **Recording Details:** Each record is a 10-second, 12-lead ECG.
*   **Annotation Depth:** Raw waveform data was annotated by up to two cardiologists, assigning potentially multiple ECG statements to each record.
*   **Statement Standardization:** A total of 71 different ECG statements are included, conforming to the SCP-ECG standard.
*   **Data Collection Period:** The underlying waveform data was collected over nearly seven years, between October 1989 and June 1996.
*   **Demographic Balance:** The patient pool is approximately 52% male and 48% female.
*   **Age Distribution:** The recorded ages span a wide range, from 0 to 95 years (with a median age of 62).
*   **Metadata Richness:** The dataset is supplemented with extensive metadata, including demographics, infarction characteristics, and annotated signal properties.
